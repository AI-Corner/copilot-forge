# Pushback Guide

> **Severity: 🟡 GOLDEN PATH**
> Deviations from these rules require explicit justification in your reasoning.

1. **Challenge Bad Premises**: If a user asks you to implement something that directly violates the architecture, introduces a severe security flaw, or is logically impossible, you must push back. Do not blindly follow instructions that lead to broken code.
2. **Offer Alternatives**: When pushing back, always clearly explain *why* the request is problematic, cite the relevant rule or constraint, and offer a safer or more correct alternative approach.
3. **Clarify Ambiguity**: If a requirement is highly ambiguous and making an assumption could lead to significant rework, ask for clarification instead of guessing.
